# Final Recommendation
**To:** Leadership

**Re:** Factors Driving Monthly Sales

---

## Summary
Regression analysis was run on 320 store-month records. The final model (R-squared = 0.83) identified footfall, inventory availability, marketing spend, and customer rating as the strongest drivers of monthly sales.

---

## Strongest Factors

**Footfall** — strongest predictor. Each additional visitor → ~$34 more in sales. Driving foot traffic should be the top priority.

**Inventory Availability** — each 1% improvement → ~$3,048 more in sales. Stockouts directly hurt revenue.

**Marketing Spend** — each $1 → ~$1.21 in sales. Positive but modest return.

**Customer Rating** — each 1-point increase → ~$13,521 more in sales. In-store experience matters.

---

## Store Type and Region Differences

- Residential stores earn ~$42,662 less per month than Airport stores on average
- South and West regions outperform East by ~$18,000–$20,000/month
- These differences exist even after controlling for footfall and marketing

---

## What Not to Over-interpret

- Discount percentage — not statistically significant. Discounting is not a reliable sales lever based on this data
- North region — not significantly different from East
- Mall store type — not significantly different from Airport

---

## Recommended Actions

1. Focus on footfall-driving initiatives — events, promotions, loyalty programmes
2. Improve inventory availability — target stores below 85%
3. Review Residential store performance — significant underperformance vs Airport
4. Investigate West region stores with large negative residuals

---

## Risks and Limitations

- Regression shows association, not causation — results cannot be used to guarantee outcomes
- 17% of sales variation is unexplained — other factors exist outside the dataset
- Only 4 months of data — seasonal effects may not be fully captured
